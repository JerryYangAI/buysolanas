## Slippage

**Slippage** is the difference between the price you expect to pay for a token swap and the actual price you receive when the trade executes. It commonly occurs in decentralized trading due to price changes between the time you submit a trade and when it is processed, or because of insufficient liquidity in the pool.

### How It Works

When you place a large trade on a DEX relative to the pool's liquidity, the AMM formula shifts the price significantly as you buy or sell. For example, if a pool has $100,000 in liquidity and you try to trade $10,000, you might move the price by several percent. Most DEXs let you set a "slippage tolerance" — the maximum price difference you are willing to accept. If slippage exceeds your tolerance, the transaction is cancelled.

### Why It Matters

Understanding slippage is essential for efficient trading on Solana. High slippage means you are getting fewer tokens than expected. You can reduce slippage by trading on pools with deep liquidity, using aggregators like Jupiter that split trades across multiple pools, or breaking large trades into smaller ones. Setting appropriate slippage tolerance protects you from unexpected losses while ensuring your trades complete successfully.
