## Treasury

A **treasury** in crypto refers to a pool of assets controlled by a protocol or DAO. These funds are typically used to finance development, fund grants, provide liquidity incentives, and support the long-term sustainability of the project.

### How It Works

Protocol treasuries accumulate funds through various mechanisms: a percentage of transaction fees, token allocations at launch, or revenue from protocol operations. Spending from the treasury is usually governed by the DAO through proposal and voting processes. Multi-signature wallets or on-chain governance contracts typically secure treasury funds.

### Why It Matters

A well-managed treasury ensures the long-term viability of a protocol. It funds ongoing development, attracts contributors through grants, and can be used to respond to emergencies. The size and management of a treasury are important factors when evaluating a project's sustainability.
