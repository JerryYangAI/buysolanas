## Full Node

A **full node** is a computer that downloads, stores, and validates the entire blockchain history from the genesis block to the latest. It independently verifies every transaction against the consensus rules without trusting any third party.

### How It Works

Full nodes synchronize the complete blockchain state by processing every historical block and transaction. They validate that all rules have been followed and maintain an up-to-date copy of the entire ledger. While resource-intensive (requiring significant storage and bandwidth), they provide the strongest security guarantees.

### Why It Matters

Full nodes are the ultimate source of truth on a blockchain. Running a full node means you never need to trust anyone else's data — you verify everything yourself. They are essential for network health and decentralization, though most regular users rely on lighter alternatives for everyday interactions.
