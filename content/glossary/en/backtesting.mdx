## Backtesting

**Backtesting** is the process of applying a trading strategy to historical market data to evaluate how it would have performed in the past. It helps traders validate their strategies before risking real capital.

### How It Works

Traders define their strategy rules (entry conditions, exit conditions, position sizing) and run them against historical price data. The backtest calculates metrics like total return, maximum drawdown, win rate, and risk-adjusted performance. Results help identify whether a strategy is viable and how it performs under different market conditions.

### Why It Matters

Backtesting provides evidence-based confidence in a trading strategy before deployment. A strategy that performs poorly on historical data is unlikely to succeed in live markets. However, traders should be aware of overfitting — optimizing too heavily for past data can create strategies that fail in new market conditions.
