## Hardware Wallet

A **hardware wallet** is a physical device that stores your private keys offline in a secure chip, completely isolated from internet-connected devices. Popular examples include Ledger and Trezor.

### How It Works

Hardware wallets generate and store private keys inside a tamper-resistant chip that never exposes them to your computer or the internet. When signing a transaction, you physically approve it on the device. The key itself never leaves the device.

### Why It Matters

Hardware wallets provide the strongest protection against online threats including malware, phishing, and remote hacking. Even if your computer is compromised, attackers cannot access your private keys. They are strongly recommended for securing significant crypto holdings.
