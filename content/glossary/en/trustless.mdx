## Trustless

**Trustless** describes a system where participants do not need to trust each other or any intermediary to transact safely. Instead, trust is placed in cryptographic proofs, smart contract code, and consensus mechanisms.

### How It Works

In a trustless system, smart contracts enforce rules automatically and transparently. When you swap tokens on a DEX, you do not need to trust the DEX operator — the smart contract guarantees the swap happens correctly or not at all. Blockchain consensus ensures that validators cannot cheat, and cryptographic proofs verify data integrity.

### Why It Matters

Trustlessness eliminates counterparty risk and the need for trusted intermediaries. This enables global, borderless transactions between strangers with the same security guarantees as transactions between trusted parties. It is the foundation that makes DeFi, NFT trading, and other blockchain applications possible.
