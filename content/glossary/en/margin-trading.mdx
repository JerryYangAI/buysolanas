## Margin Trading

**Margin trading** involves borrowing funds from a protocol or exchange to trade with a larger position than your own capital would allow. The borrowed amount is your "margin," and your deposited funds serve as collateral.

### How It Works

Traders deposit collateral and borrow additional funds to open larger positions. For example, with 2x margin, a $1,000 deposit lets you trade with $2,000. Profits and losses are amplified by the margin ratio. If losses approach your collateral value, your position is automatically liquidated. Interest is charged on borrowed funds.

### Why It Matters

Margin trading allows experienced traders to amplify returns and employ advanced strategies. However, it significantly increases risk through the possibility of liquidation. On Solana, DeFi protocols enable margin trading in a decentralized manner, but users should fully understand the mechanics and risks before using leverage.
