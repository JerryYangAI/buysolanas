## APY (Annual Percentage Yield)

**Annual Percentage Yield (APY)** represents the real rate of return earned on a crypto deposit or investment over one year, taking into account the effect of compounding interest. It is the most common metric used to compare yields across different DeFi protocols and investment opportunities.

### How It Works

APY differs from a simple interest rate because it accounts for compounding — meaning the interest you earn also earns interest over time. For example, a 10% APY means that if you deposit $1,000, you would have approximately $1,100 after one year (with the actual amount slightly higher due to compounding). In DeFi, APY can come from trading fees, token rewards, or staking yields.

### Why It Matters

When comparing DeFi opportunities, APY gives you a standardized way to evaluate potential returns. However, be cautious of extremely high APYs — they often come with higher risk, may be unsustainable, or may be denominated in volatile tokens. On Solana, staking SOL typically offers 6-8% APY, while liquidity provision and yield farming can offer higher but variable returns.
