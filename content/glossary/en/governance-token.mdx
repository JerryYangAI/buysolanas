## Governance Token

A **governance token** grants holders the right to vote on decisions affecting a decentralized protocol. These decisions can include protocol upgrades, fee structures, treasury allocations, and strategic direction.

### How It Works

Governance tokens work through on-chain voting mechanisms. Token holders create proposals and vote, with voting power typically proportional to token holdings. Some protocols use delegation, allowing holders to assign their voting power to representatives. Proposals that reach quorum and majority approval are executed, often automatically through smart contracts.

### Why It Matters

Governance tokens are a key mechanism for decentralization. Instead of a company making decisions, the community of token holders collectively governs the protocol. This aligns incentives between users and the protocol, though voter participation and governance attack risks remain ongoing challenges.
