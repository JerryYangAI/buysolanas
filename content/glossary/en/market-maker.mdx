## Market Maker

A **market maker** is a participant (individual, firm, or algorithm) that provides liquidity to a market by continuously placing both buy and sell orders. They profit from the difference between their buy price (bid) and sell price (ask) — the spread.

### How It Works

Market makers quote prices on both sides of the order book, ensuring there is always someone willing to buy and sell. In traditional finance, these are specialized firms. In DeFi, market making can be automated through AMMs (where liquidity providers serve this role) or through professional market makers running bots on on-chain order books.

### Why It Matters

Market makers are essential for liquid, efficient markets. Without them, traders would face wide spreads and difficulty executing orders. On Solana, the combination of low fees and fast block times makes it attractive for professional market makers, resulting in tighter spreads and better execution for all users.
