## Algorithmic Stablecoin

An **algorithmic stablecoin** attempts to maintain a stable price (usually $1) through automated supply and demand mechanisms rather than holding reserves of fiat currency or crypto collateral.

### How It Works

Algorithmic stablecoins use various mechanisms to maintain their peg. Some use seigniorage models with companion tokens, where the protocol mints or burns supply based on demand. Others use fractional collateral combined with algorithmic stabilization. When the price drifts above the peg, new tokens are minted; below the peg, tokens are incentivized to be burned or removed.

### Why It Matters

Algorithmic stablecoins represent an ambitious attempt at creating truly decentralized stable money. However, the collapse of UST/Luna demonstrated the risks — algorithmic models can enter death spirals during severe market stress. Most experts now view fully algorithmic stablecoins as inherently fragile without sufficient collateral backing.
