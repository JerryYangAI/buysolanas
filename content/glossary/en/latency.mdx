## Latency

**Latency** in blockchain refers to the time delay between when a user submits a transaction and when it is confirmed and finalized on the network. Lower latency means faster, more responsive user experiences.

### How It Works

Latency includes network propagation time, time waiting in the queue for block inclusion, and the block production/confirmation process itself. Solana achieves very low latency — typically under 1 second for confirmation — due to its continuous block production model and fast consensus. Other chains may have latency of seconds to minutes.

### Why It Matters

Low latency is critical for time-sensitive applications. Trading, gaming, and real-time interactions require near-instant feedback. Solana's low latency makes it competitive with traditional web applications and is essential for use cases like high-frequency trading and interactive gaming.
