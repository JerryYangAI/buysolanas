## HODL

**HODL** is one of the most iconic terms in cryptocurrency culture. Originally a typo for "hold" in a 2013 Bitcoin forum post, it has since become a rallying cry for long-term investors who refuse to sell their crypto regardless of market volatility. It is sometimes backronymed as "Hold On for Dear Life."

### Origin and Usage

The term originated from a now-famous Bitcoin Talk forum post where a user, during a market crash, typed "I AM HODLING" instead of "I AM HOLDING." The community embraced the typo, and it became a meme that represents the strategy of holding through market downturns. When someone says "HODL," they mean they are committed to keeping their position no matter what happens to the price.

### Why It Matters

HODL represents a fundamental investment philosophy in crypto: the belief that despite short-term volatility, the long-term trajectory of quality crypto assets is upward. It is the opposite of panic selling during market crashes. While HODLing can be a sound strategy for established cryptocurrencies, it is important to distinguish between conviction-based holding and blindly holding assets that may not recover. Always do your own research and have a clear investment thesis.
