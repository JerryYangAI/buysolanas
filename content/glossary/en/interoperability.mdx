## Interoperability

**Interoperability** refers to the ability of different blockchain networks to communicate, share data, and transfer assets between each other. It is the technical foundation for a connected multi-chain ecosystem.

### How It Works

Interoperability is achieved through bridge protocols, cross-chain messaging layers, and standardized communication protocols. Solutions like Wormhole enable Solana to communicate with Ethereum, BNB Chain, and other networks. More advanced interoperability protocols support arbitrary cross-chain smart contract calls, not just token transfers.

### Why It Matters

No single blockchain can serve all use cases optimally. Interoperability allows users and developers to leverage the strengths of different chains while maintaining connectivity. It prevents ecosystem fragmentation and ensures that liquidity and users can flow freely across the blockchain landscape.
