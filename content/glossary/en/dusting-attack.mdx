## Dusting Attack

A **dusting attack** involves sending minuscule amounts of cryptocurrency (dust) to a large number of wallets. The goal is to track subsequent transactions to link wallet addresses to real-world identities, or to trick users into interacting with malicious tokens.

### How It Works

Attackers send tiny token amounts to thousands of wallets. If recipients move these tokens, the attacker can analyze the transaction trail to cluster addresses belonging to the same person. In some cases, the dust tokens themselves are malicious — interacting with them can trigger approvals that drain your wallet.

### Why It Matters

Dusting attacks threaten privacy and can be a precursor to targeted phishing. The best defense is to ignore unknown tokens that appear in your wallet. Do not attempt to swap or transfer them, as this could expose you to further attacks or trigger hidden malicious contracts.
