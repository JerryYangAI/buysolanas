## Vesting

**Vesting** is a mechanism that gradually releases tokens to recipients over a predetermined schedule rather than all at once. It is commonly applied to team allocations, investor tokens, and advisor shares to align long-term incentives.

### How It Works

A vesting schedule defines when and how tokens become available. Typically, there is a cliff period (e.g., 6-12 months) during which no tokens are released, followed by a gradual linear or periodic unlock. For example, a 4-year vest with a 1-year cliff means no tokens for the first year, then monthly releases over the remaining 3 years.

### Why It Matters

Vesting protects token holders from immediate dumps by insiders. When team members and investors have vesting schedules, they are incentivized to contribute to the project's long-term success rather than selling immediately after launch. Checking vesting schedules is crucial when evaluating a token's potential selling pressure.
