## AMM (Automated Market Maker)

An **Automated Market Maker (AMM)** is a type of decentralized exchange mechanism that uses mathematical formulas and liquidity pools to determine token prices, rather than relying on traditional buy and sell order books. AMMs are the engine behind most decentralized exchanges.

### How It Works

An AMM uses a liquidity pool containing two tokens (e.g., SOL and USDC). The price of each token is determined by the ratio of the two tokens in the pool. When you buy token A, you add token B to the pool and remove token A, which changes the ratio and adjusts the price. The most common formula is x * y = k, where x and y are the quantities of each token and k is a constant.

### Why It Matters

AMMs revolutionized crypto trading by making it possible to trade tokens without needing a counterparty on the other side. Anyone can become a liquidity provider by depositing tokens into a pool and earning fees from trades. On Solana, AMMs like Raydium and Orca power billions of dollars in daily trading volume with sub-second transaction speeds.
