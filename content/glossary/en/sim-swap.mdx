## SIM Swap

A **SIM swap** attack occurs when an attacker convinces your mobile carrier to transfer your phone number to a SIM card they control. This allows them to intercept SMS-based two-factor authentication codes and gain access to your accounts.

### How It Works

Attackers gather personal information about the victim through social media, data breaches, or social engineering. They contact the mobile carrier, impersonate the victim, and request a SIM transfer. Once successful, all calls and texts go to the attacker's device, including 2FA codes for exchanges and email accounts.

### Why It Matters

SIM swaps have led to significant crypto thefts. This is why SMS-based 2FA is considered weak security. Using authenticator apps or hardware security keys instead of SMS for 2FA greatly reduces this risk. Never use SMS as your only second factor for accounts holding crypto.
