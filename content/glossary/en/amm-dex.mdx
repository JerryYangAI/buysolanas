## AMM DEX

An **AMM DEX** (Automated Market Maker Decentralized Exchange) uses liquidity pools and mathematical formulas rather than traditional order books to facilitate trades. Prices are determined by the ratio of tokens in the pool.

### How It Works

Liquidity providers deposit pairs of tokens into pools. When a trader swaps one token for another, the AMM formula (typically x * y = k) calculates the price based on the pool's current token ratios. Larger trades relative to pool size cause more slippage. LPs earn trading fees proportional to their share of the pool.

### Why It Matters

AMM DEXs democratized market making by allowing anyone to provide liquidity, not just professional firms. They are the backbone of DeFi trading on Solana, with Raydium and Orca being prominent examples. Their permissionless nature means any token can have a trading pair without needing centralized listing approval.
