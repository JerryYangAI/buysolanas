## Phishing

**Phishing** in the crypto world refers to deceptive attacks designed to trick you into giving up your private keys, seed phrases, or signing malicious transactions that drain your wallet. Attackers impersonate legitimate projects, wallets, or support teams to gain your trust.

### Common Phishing Methods

Crypto phishing takes many forms: fake websites that look identical to real DEXs or wallet sites, scam emails or DMs claiming you won an airdrop and need to connect your wallet, fake customer support accounts on social media, malicious smart contract approvals that give attackers access to your tokens, and fake browser extensions mimicking popular wallets. The goal is always the same — to steal your assets.

### Why It Matters

Phishing is the number one way crypto users lose their funds. Unlike traditional banking, blockchain transactions are irreversible — once your tokens are stolen, they are gone forever. Protect yourself by always double-checking URLs, never clicking links from unsolicited messages, never sharing your seed phrase, and being skeptical of anything that seems too good to be true. Bookmark the official websites of the services you use and always navigate to them directly.
