## TWAP (Time-Weighted Average Price)

**TWAP** stands for Time-Weighted Average Price, a trading execution strategy that breaks a large order into smaller equal portions and executes them at regular time intervals to achieve an average price over the period.

### How It Works

A TWAP order divides the total quantity equally across a set number of time intervals. For example, buying 100 SOL over 10 hours means executing 10 SOL purchases every hour regardless of the current price. The final average price represents the time-weighted average of all the individual trades.

### Why It Matters

TWAP strategies minimize market impact from large orders. By spreading execution over time, they avoid moving the price significantly against the trader. This is particularly useful for institutional-sized trades or when a trader wants to accumulate or distribute a large position without alerting the market.
