## Smart Contract

A **smart contract** is a program stored on a blockchain that automatically executes predetermined actions when specified conditions are met. On Solana, smart contracts are called "programs" and are written primarily in Rust.

### How It Works

Smart contracts contain logic that defines rules and automatically enforces them. For example, a DEX smart contract can automatically swap tokens when a user submits the right transaction. Once deployed, the contract code is immutable (unless designed to be upgradeable) and executes exactly as written, without the need for human intervention.

### Why It Matters

Smart contracts enable trustless, automated interactions between parties who do not know or trust each other. They are the foundation of DeFi, NFTs, and all blockchain applications. On Solana, smart contracts benefit from fast execution and low costs, making complex on-chain applications practical.
