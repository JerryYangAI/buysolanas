## Rollup

A **rollup** is a Layer 2 scaling solution that executes transactions off-chain, bundles them into batches, and posts compressed data or proofs back to the Layer 1 blockchain. The L1 verifies the batch's validity, inheriting its security.

### How It Works

There are two main types. Optimistic rollups assume transactions are valid and only run computation if someone challenges them (fraud proof). Zero-knowledge rollups generate a cryptographic proof that all transactions in the batch are valid (validity proof). Both dramatically increase throughput while maintaining L1 security guarantees.

### Why It Matters

Rollups are the primary scaling strategy for Ethereum, allowing it to serve more users at lower costs. While Solana's L1 already provides high throughput, understanding rollups is important for grasping the broader blockchain landscape and cross-chain interactions.
