## Futures

**Futures** are derivative contracts that obligate the buyer to purchase, and the seller to sell, an asset at a predetermined price on a specific future date. Unlike perpetual contracts, traditional futures have an expiration date.

### How It Works

Futures contracts specify an asset, price, quantity, and expiration date. Traders take long (buy) or short (sell) positions, and the contract settles at expiration. Most crypto futures are cash-settled, meaning the difference between the contract price and the market price at expiration is paid rather than delivering the actual asset.

### Why It Matters

Futures enable hedging and speculation on future prices. Miners and projects can lock in future selling prices, while traders can speculate on price direction with leverage. In crypto, perpetual contracts (no expiration) have become more popular than traditional futures, but understanding both is valuable for comprehensive market participation.
