## Throughput

**Throughput** measures the number of transactions a blockchain can process per second (TPS). It is one of the most important metrics for evaluating a blockchain's scalability and practical utility.

### How It Works

Throughput is determined by block size, block time, and transaction processing efficiency. Bitcoin processes roughly 7 TPS, Ethereum around 15-30 TPS, while Solana can handle thousands of TPS due to its parallel transaction processing via Sealevel and fast block times. Higher throughput means more users and applications can operate simultaneously.

### Why It Matters

Low throughput leads to network congestion, high fees, and poor user experience. Solana's high throughput is a key advantage, enabling fast, low-cost transactions that support real-time applications like trading, gaming, and payments at scale.
