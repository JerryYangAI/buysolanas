## Social Engineering

**Social engineering** in crypto refers to psychological manipulation tactics used to trick individuals into compromising their own security — revealing seed phrases, clicking malicious links, or approving fraudulent transactions.

### How It Works

Attackers impersonate trusted entities through social media, Discord, Telegram, or email. Common tactics include fake airdrops requiring wallet connections, urgent messages about account security, and impersonation of known figures. The goal is always to gain access to private keys or trick victims into signing malicious transactions.

### Why It Matters

Social engineering is the most common attack vector in crypto. Technical security measures like hardware wallets cannot protect against a user who willingly shares their seed phrase. Education and skepticism are the primary defenses.
