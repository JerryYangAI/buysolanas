## Buyback

A **buyback** occurs when a crypto protocol uses its revenue or treasury funds to purchase its own token from the open market. Often combined with token burns, buybacks create buying pressure and reduce circulating supply.

### How It Works

Protocols that generate revenue (from trading fees, interest, or other sources) can allocate a portion to buy their governance token on DEXs. The purchased tokens may be burned (permanently removed), distributed to stakers, or returned to the treasury. Buybacks are typically executed automatically through smart contracts or periodically by the DAO.

### Why It Matters

Buybacks directly link protocol revenue to token value, creating a fundamental value driver similar to stock buybacks in traditional finance. They signal that the protocol generates real revenue and returns value to token holders, making the token more than just a speculative asset.
