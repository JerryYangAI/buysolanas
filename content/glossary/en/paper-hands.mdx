## Paper Hands

**Paper hands** describes investors who sell their positions quickly at the first sign of a price decline, lacking the conviction to hold through volatility. It is the opposite of "diamond hands."

### How It Works

A paper-handed investor might buy a token, see a 10-20% price drop, panic, and sell at a loss. The term is often used critically by community members who believe in holding through dips. Someone selling during normal market volatility — only to watch the price recover and surge — is the classic paper hands scenario.

### Why It Matters

While "paper hands" is used negatively, selling to manage risk is sometimes the correct decision. The important distinction is between panic selling (emotional, reactive) and strategic selling (planned, based on analysis). Setting clear investment theses and exit strategies beforehand helps avoid both paper-handing and stubborn holding.
