## Allowlist

An **allowlist** is the preferred modern term for what was traditionally called a whitelist. It refers to a pre-approved set of wallet addresses that receive early or guaranteed access to mint NFTs, participate in token sales, or access exclusive features.

### How It Works

Allowlists function identically to whitelists. Projects compile a list of approved wallet addresses, often using Merkle trees for efficient on-chain verification. During the mint, the smart contract checks whether the minting address is in the allowlist before permitting the transaction.

### Why It Matters

The term "allowlist" has been adopted by much of the crypto community as a more inclusive alternative to "whitelist." Functionally, the mechanism serves the same purpose: ensuring fair access for community members, preventing bot manipulation, and rewarding early supporters of a project.
