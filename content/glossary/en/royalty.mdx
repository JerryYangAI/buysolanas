## Royalty

A **royalty** in the NFT context is a percentage of the sale price that is paid to the original creator every time the NFT is resold on a secondary marketplace. This mechanism allows artists and creators to earn ongoing income from their work as it appreciates in value and changes hands.

### How It Works

When a creator mints an NFT collection, they set a royalty percentage (typically 2-10%) in the NFT's metadata. Each time that NFT is sold on a supporting marketplace, the smart contract automatically calculates and sends the royalty payment to the creator's wallet. On Solana, the Metaplex standard supports royalty enforcement, though some marketplaces have made royalties optional for buyers.

### Why It Matters

Royalties were one of the most revolutionary aspects of NFTs for creators. In traditional art markets, an artist only profits from the initial sale. With NFT royalties, creators can benefit from the long-term success of their work. However, the enforcement of royalties has been a contentious topic in the Solana ecosystem, with some marketplaces choosing not to enforce them to attract more traders with lower costs.
