## Yield Farming

**Yield farming** is a DeFi strategy where users deposit or "stake" their crypto assets in various protocols to earn returns. These returns can come from trading fees, interest on lending, or bonus token rewards distributed by the protocol. Yield farmers actively move their assets between opportunities to maximize their overall yield.

### How It Works

A yield farmer might deposit SOL and USDC into a Raydium liquidity pool, earning trading fees. They then take the LP tokens they receive and stake them in a reward program for additional token incentives. Some protocols offer tiered rewards that increase when you lock your assets for longer periods. The combined returns from all these layers stack up to create a higher overall APY.

### Why It Matters

Yield farming introduced the concept of earning returns on idle crypto assets, much like earning interest at a bank but often at significantly higher rates. However, it comes with risks including impermanent loss, smart contract vulnerabilities, and the possibility that reward token values may decline. On Solana, the low transaction fees make yield farming more accessible since frequent rebalancing does not eat into profits.
