## Transaction Fee

A **transaction fee** is a small amount paid to the network for processing and confirming your transaction. Fees compensate validators for their computational work and prevent network spam.

### How It Works

On Solana, the base transaction fee is fixed at 5,000 lamports (0.000005 SOL) per signature, making it extremely affordable. Users can optionally add priority fees for faster inclusion during congestion. A portion of fees is burned (removed from circulation) and the rest goes to the validator that processed the transaction.

### Why It Matters

Solana's consistently low transaction fees are one of its biggest advantages. While Ethereum fees can spike to tens or hundreds of dollars during congestion, Solana fees typically remain under a cent, making it practical for microtransactions, frequent trading, and everyday use cases that would be prohibitively expensive on higher-fee chains.
