## Mint

**Minting** is the process of creating a new digital asset — whether an NFT, token, or other on-chain item — on the blockchain for the first time. When you mint an NFT, you are writing its data to the blockchain, creating a permanent, verifiable record of its existence and your ownership.

### How It Works

For NFTs, minting involves sending a transaction to the blockchain that creates a new token with associated metadata (name, image, attributes). On Solana, this is typically done through the Metaplex protocol. The creator pays a small transaction fee, and the NFT is assigned to their wallet. For token launches, minting refers to creating the initial supply of a new cryptocurrency token.

### Why It Matters

Minting is the birth of every digital asset on the blockchain. For NFT collectors, participating in a mint event means buying an NFT at its initial price, often before its market value is established. Mint prices on Solana are typically very affordable due to the chain's low fees. However, popular mints can be highly competitive, with thousands of users trying to mint at the same time, which is why Solana's speed and low costs give it an advantage.
