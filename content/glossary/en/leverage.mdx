## Leverage

**Leverage** means using borrowed capital to increase the size of a trading position beyond what your own funds would allow. In crypto, leverage is expressed as a multiplier — 2x leverage means you control twice the position size with your capital.

### How It Works

In DeFi, leverage can be achieved through lending protocols (borrow assets, sell them, and repeat) or through perpetual futures exchanges that offer built-in leverage. For example, with 5x leverage and $1,000, you control a $5,000 position. If the price moves 10% in your favor, you gain $500 (50% return). But if it moves 10% against you, you lose $500.

### Why It Matters

Leverage amplifies both gains and losses, making it a powerful but risky tool. While it can significantly increase profits in favorable conditions, it also increases liquidation risk. New traders should approach leverage cautiously and understand margin requirements and liquidation mechanics before using it.
