## Decentralization

**Decentralization** means distributing power, control, and decision-making across many participants rather than concentrating it in a single entity. In blockchain, it means no single party controls the network, its data, or its rules.

### How It Works

Blockchains achieve decentralization through distributed networks of nodes and validators. No single entity can unilaterally change the rules, censor transactions, or shut down the network. Governance decisions are made through community voting rather than corporate boards. The degree of decentralization varies across blockchains and is measured by validator count, stake distribution, and governance mechanisms.

### Why It Matters

Decentralization provides censorship resistance, fault tolerance, and trustless operation. It ensures no single point of failure and no single entity can control users' assets. While full decentralization involves trade-offs with performance, it is the core value proposition that distinguishes blockchain from traditional systems.
