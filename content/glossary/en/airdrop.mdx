## Airdrop

An **airdrop** is a marketing and distribution strategy where a crypto project sends free tokens or NFTs directly to users' wallet addresses. Airdrops are commonly used to reward early adopters, incentivize participation, or distribute governance tokens to a protocol's community.

### How It Works

Projects typically take a "snapshot" of wallet activity at a specific point in time and create a list of eligible addresses based on criteria like trading volume, liquidity provision, or simply holding certain tokens. Eligible users can then "claim" their airdrop through the project's website or it may arrive automatically. Some of the largest Solana airdrops (like Jupiter's JUP token) have been worth thousands of dollars per recipient.

### Why It Matters

Airdrops have become one of the most exciting aspects of the crypto ecosystem. Active DeFi users who interact with new protocols may qualify for valuable token distributions when those protocols launch their governance tokens. This has created a culture of "airdrop farming" where users actively try protocols hoping to qualify for future airdrops. However, be cautious of scam airdrops — never connect your wallet to suspicious claiming sites.
