## Gas Fee

A **gas fee** is the cost you pay to have your transaction processed and validated on a blockchain network. The term originated with Ethereum but is broadly used across blockchains. Gas fees compensate validators for the computational resources needed to execute transactions and secure the network.

### How It Works

Every blockchain operation — sending tokens, swapping on a DEX, minting an NFT — requires computational work from validators. Gas fees are the payment for this work. On Ethereum, gas fees fluctuate wildly based on network congestion, sometimes costing tens or hundreds of dollars for a single transaction. On Solana, fees are fixed at a fraction of a cent (typically around $0.00025 per transaction), making it dramatically more affordable.

### Why It Matters

Gas fees directly impact usability. High fees on Ethereum have priced out many users, especially for small transactions. Solana's consistently low fees are one of its biggest advantages, enabling use cases like micropayments, frequent trading, and gaming that would be impractical on higher-fee chains. When using Solana, you need to keep a small amount of SOL in your wallet to cover these minimal fees.
