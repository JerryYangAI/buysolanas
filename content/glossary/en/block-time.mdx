## Block Time

**Block time** is the average duration between the production of consecutive blocks on a blockchain. It determines how quickly new transactions can be included in the chain and is a key performance metric.

### How It Works

Each blockchain has a target block time determined by its consensus mechanism. Bitcoin targets 10 minutes, Ethereum around 12 seconds, and Solana approximately 400 milliseconds. Shorter block times mean faster transaction inclusion but may require trade-offs in other areas like bandwidth and storage requirements.

### Why It Matters

Block time directly affects user experience. Solana's sub-second block time means transactions are included almost instantly, making it feel like a traditional application. Longer block times on other chains create noticeable delays that can impact trading, gaming, and payment use cases.
