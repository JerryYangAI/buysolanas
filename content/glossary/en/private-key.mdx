## Private Key

A **private key** is a secret cryptographic string of numbers and letters that gives you full control over a specific blockchain address and all the assets it holds. It is the digital equivalent of a signature that authorizes every transaction you make. Anyone who possesses your private key can spend your funds.

### How It Works

Every blockchain wallet address has a corresponding private key. When you send a transaction, your wallet uses the private key to create a digital signature that proves you authorized the transfer. The network can verify this signature without ever seeing the private key itself. Your seed phrase generates all your private keys, so a single seed phrase can control multiple addresses.

### Why It Matters

Understanding the difference between public and private keys is fundamental to crypto security. Your public address (wallet address) can be shared freely — it is like an email address. Your private key must never be shared — it is like the password that gives full access to your account. Unlike traditional banking, there is no customer support to help if your private key is compromised. Self-custody means full responsibility.
