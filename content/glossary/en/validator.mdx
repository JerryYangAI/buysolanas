## Validator

A **validator** is a computer that participates in running the Solana blockchain network. Validators process transactions, produce new blocks, vote on the state of the ledger, and help maintain the network's security and integrity. They are the backbone of Solana's decentralized infrastructure.

### How It Works

Validators run specialized software that receives transactions, verifies their validity, and includes them in new blocks. They participate in consensus through Tower BFT, voting on which transactions are final. Validators earn rewards from transaction fees and inflation-based staking rewards. To become a validator, you need high-performance hardware and sufficient SOL staked (either your own or delegated by others).

### Why It Matters

The health of any blockchain depends on having a large, diverse set of validators. More validators means more decentralization, which makes the network more resistant to censorship and attacks. When you stake your SOL, you are choosing which validator to support. Picking reliable validators with good uptime and reasonable commission rates helps keep the network healthy while maximizing your staking returns.
