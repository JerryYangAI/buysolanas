## NGMI

**NGMI** stands for "Not Gonna Make It." It is used in the crypto community to describe actions, decisions, or attitudes that are likely to lead to losses or missed opportunities. It can be used seriously or humorously, and is the opposite of WAGMI (We're All Gonna Make It).

### Usage and Context

NGMI is typically used in three ways: to criticize bad investment decisions (like selling right before a major price increase), to mock people who dismiss crypto entirely, or as self-deprecating humor when someone realizes they made a mistake. For example, someone who sold their SOL at the bottom of a dip might say "I'm NGMI" as a way of acknowledging their poor timing.

### Why It Matters

While NGMI might seem like just internet slang, it reflects the crypto community's way of processing risk and decision-making. It serves as informal peer feedback — when the community says a particular action is "NGMI," it often highlights genuinely risky behavior like ignoring security practices, falling for obvious scams, or making emotional trading decisions. Understanding this term helps you read community sentiment and learn from others' mistakes.
