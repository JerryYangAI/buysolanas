## DeFi (Decentralized Finance)

**DeFi** stands for Decentralized Finance, a movement that recreates traditional financial services — lending, borrowing, trading, insurance, and more — using blockchain technology and smart contracts instead of banks, brokerages, and other intermediaries.

### How It Works

DeFi applications (called protocols or dApps) run on blockchains like Solana and Ethereum. They use smart contracts — self-executing code — to automate financial operations. For example, instead of a bank deciding whether to approve your loan, a DeFi lending protocol automatically matches lenders with borrowers based on transparent rules encoded in the smart contract.

### Why It Matters

DeFi is open to anyone with an internet connection and a crypto wallet — no credit checks, no minimum balances, no geographic restrictions. It operates 24/7 and offers transparency since all transactions are visible on the blockchain. On Solana, DeFi is particularly attractive because transactions are fast and fees are minimal, making it practical even for small amounts.
