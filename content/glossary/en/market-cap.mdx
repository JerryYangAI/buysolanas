## Market Cap (Market Capitalization)

**Market cap** is the total market value of a cryptocurrency, calculated by multiplying the current token price by the number of tokens in circulation. It is the most common metric for comparing the relative size of different crypto projects.

### How It Works

Market cap = Current Price x Circulating Supply. For example, if SOL trades at $100 and there are 400 million SOL in circulation, the market cap is $40 billion. Market caps are often categorized as large-cap (over $10B), mid-cap ($1-10B), and small-cap (under $1B).

### Why It Matters

Market cap provides a quick way to gauge a project's size and relative market position. However, it should not be used in isolation — a high market cap does not guarantee quality, and a low market cap does not necessarily mean opportunity. Always consider market cap alongside other metrics like TVL, revenue, and user activity.
