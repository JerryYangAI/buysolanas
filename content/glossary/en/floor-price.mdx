## Floor Price

The **floor price** is the lowest price at which any NFT in a specific collection is currently listed for sale. It represents the minimum entry cost to own an NFT from that collection and is the most commonly used metric for tracking an NFT project's overall market value and demand.

### How It Works

In an NFT collection of 10,000 items, each owner can list their NFT at whatever price they choose. The floor price is simply the cheapest listing available at any given moment. If the lowest-priced NFT in a collection is listed at 5 SOL, that is the floor price. When someone buys that NFT, the floor price becomes whatever the next cheapest listing is.

### Why It Matters

Floor price is a quick way to gauge market sentiment for an NFT project. A rising floor price suggests growing demand, while a falling floor suggests decreasing interest or holders selling off. Traders and collectors watch floor prices closely on platforms like Magic Eden and Tensor. However, floor price alone does not tell the whole story — factors like trading volume, unique holders, and the rarity of specific items also matter.
