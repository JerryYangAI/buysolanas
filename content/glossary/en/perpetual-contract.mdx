## Perpetual Contract

A **perpetual contract** (or "perp") is a type of futures contract with no expiration date. It allows traders to speculate on the price of an asset with leverage, going long (betting price goes up) or short (betting price goes down) indefinitely.

### How It Works

Perpetual contracts use a funding rate mechanism to keep the contract price aligned with the spot price. When the perp price trades above the spot price, longs pay shorts. When it trades below, shorts pay longs. This creates an incentive for the price to converge. Traders post margin as collateral and can be liquidated if the market moves against them.

### Why It Matters

Perps are the most traded crypto derivative instrument by volume. They allow sophisticated trading strategies including hedging, speculation, and arbitrage without needing to own the underlying asset. On Solana, platforms like Drift and Jupiter Perps offer decentralized perps trading.
