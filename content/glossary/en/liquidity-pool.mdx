## Liquidity Pool

A **liquidity pool** is a collection of cryptocurrency tokens locked in a smart contract that provides the liquidity needed for decentralized trading. Instead of matching individual buyers and sellers, traders swap against the pooled assets, and the pool's algorithm determines the price.

### How It Works

A typical liquidity pool contains two tokens in a specific ratio (e.g., SOL/USDC). Users called "liquidity providers" (LPs) deposit equal values of both tokens into the pool. When someone makes a trade, they swap one token for the other from the pool, paying a small fee (usually 0.2-0.3%). This fee is distributed to the liquidity providers as a reward for supplying their tokens.

### Why It Matters

Liquidity pools are the backbone of decentralized finance. Without them, DEXs would not be able to function. For users, becoming a liquidity provider is a way to earn passive income from trading fees. However, LPs face risks like impermanent loss, where the value of their deposited tokens can decrease relative to simply holding them. Understanding liquidity pools is essential for anyone participating in DeFi on Solana.
