## Collateral

**Collateral** in DeFi refers to crypto assets that a borrower deposits into a lending protocol as security for a loan. If the value of the collateral falls below a required threshold, it can be liquidated to repay the loan and protect lenders.

### How It Works

When borrowing in DeFi, you must provide collateral worth more than the loan amount (over-collateralization). Each asset has a collateral factor determining how much you can borrow against it. For example, a 75% collateral factor means you can borrow up to 75% of your deposit's value. The protocol continuously monitors collateral ratios.

### Why It Matters

Collateral is what makes trustless lending possible. Since there are no credit checks in DeFi, over-collateralization ensures lenders are protected. Understanding collateral requirements and liquidation thresholds is essential for anyone borrowing in DeFi to avoid unexpected losses.
