## VWAP (Volume-Weighted Average Price)

**VWAP** stands for Volume-Weighted Average Price, a trading benchmark that represents the average price an asset has traded at throughout the day, weighted by the volume traded at each price level.

### How It Works

VWAP is calculated by taking the total dollar amount traded at each price level and dividing by total volume. Price levels with higher trading volume have more influence on the VWAP. Traders use VWAP as both a benchmark (to evaluate execution quality) and a trading indicator (prices above VWAP suggest bullish sentiment, below suggest bearish).

### Why It Matters

VWAP helps traders assess whether they got a good execution price and serves as a key technical indicator. Institutional traders often aim to execute at or below VWAP when buying. It provides a more accurate picture of true average trading price compared to simple time-based averages.
