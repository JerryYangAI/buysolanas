## Sandwich Attack

A **sandwich attack** is a form of MEV extraction where an attacker places one transaction before and one after a victim's pending swap. The attacker buys before the victim (front-run), causing the price to rise, and sells after (back-run), profiting from the victim's forced worse execution price.

### How It Works

A bot detects a pending swap in the mempool and calculates the price impact. It places a buy order just before the victim's transaction (driving the price up), lets the victim's order execute at the now-higher price, then sells immediately after at the elevated price. The victim gets fewer tokens than expected, and the attacker pockets the difference.

### Why It Matters

Sandwich attacks are a persistent problem that extracts value from regular DeFi users. Using tight slippage settings, private transaction submission, and MEV-protected RPC endpoints can reduce exposure. On Solana, the fast block times make sandwiching harder but not impossible.
