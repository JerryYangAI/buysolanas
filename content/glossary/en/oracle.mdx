## Oracle

An **oracle** is a service that provides external, real-world data to blockchain smart contracts. Since blockchains cannot access off-chain information natively, oracles serve as the bridge between the external world and on-chain applications.

### How It Works

Oracle networks consist of multiple independent data providers who fetch, validate, and publish data on-chain. Common data types include asset prices, weather data, sports scores, and random numbers. The data is typically aggregated from multiple sources to prevent manipulation. On Solana, Pyth and Switchboard are the primary oracle solutions.

### Why It Matters

Oracles are critical infrastructure for DeFi. Without reliable price data, lending protocols cannot calculate collateral values, DEXs cannot offer accurate pricing, and derivatives cannot settle properly. The security and reliability of oracles directly impacts the safety of billions of dollars in DeFi protocols.
