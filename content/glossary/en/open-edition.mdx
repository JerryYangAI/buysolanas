## Open Edition

An **open edition** is an NFT release format where there is no cap on the number of NFTs that can be minted during a specified time window. Anyone can mint as many copies as they want until the window closes, and the total supply is determined by demand.

### How It Works

The creator sets a minting window (e.g., 24 hours) and a price per mint. During this window, unlimited minting is allowed. Once the window closes, no more NFTs can be created, making the final supply fixed and known. Open editions are typically priced low to maximize accessibility and participation.

### Why It Matters

Open editions democratize NFT collecting by removing supply-driven FOMO (fear of missing out). They prioritize community participation over scarcity, making art and collectibles accessible to a wider audience. The resulting supply reflects genuine demand rather than artificial scarcity.
