## Clipboard Hijacking

**Clipboard hijacking** is a type of malware attack where software monitors your clipboard and replaces copied wallet addresses with an attacker-controlled address. When you paste what you think is the recipient's address, you actually paste the attacker's.

### How It Works

The malware runs silently in the background, monitoring the clipboard for patterns matching crypto wallet addresses. When it detects a copied address, it swaps it with the attacker's address. Since wallet addresses are long strings of characters, most users do not notice the substitution and send funds to the wrong address.

### Why It Matters

Clipboard hijacking has resulted in significant crypto losses. Always double-check the first and last several characters of a pasted address before confirming any transaction. Using address books, QR codes, or verified contact features in wallets can help prevent this attack.
