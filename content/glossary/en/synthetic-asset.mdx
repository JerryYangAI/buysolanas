## Synthetic Asset

A **synthetic asset** is a tokenized derivative on the blockchain that tracks the price of another asset — such as stocks, commodities, or fiat currencies — without requiring the holder to own the actual underlying asset.

### How It Works

Synthetic assets use oracles to track the price of real-world assets and smart contracts to maintain the peg. Users typically mint synthetics by depositing collateral. For example, a synthetic gold token tracks the gold price via oracle feeds, and its value is backed by crypto collateral locked in a smart contract.

### Why It Matters

Synthetics bring the world of traditional finance on-chain, allowing anyone with a crypto wallet to gain exposure to stocks, commodities, forex, and other assets 24/7 without brokerage accounts or geographic restrictions. They expand the range of financial instruments available in DeFi.
