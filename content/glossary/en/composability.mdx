## Composability

**Composability** is the ability of blockchain applications to seamlessly interact with and build upon each other, like LEGO blocks. A DeFi protocol can use another protocol's functionality without permission, creating complex financial products from simple components.

### How It Works

Because smart contracts on the same blockchain share a common execution environment, they can call each other's functions within a single transaction. For example, a single transaction on Solana could swap tokens on Jupiter, deposit them into a lending protocol, and use the receipt token as collateral — all atomically. This is sometimes called "money LEGOs."

### Why It Matters

Composability is DeFi's superpower. It enables innovation at a pace impossible in traditional finance, where integrating systems requires lengthy partnerships and custom development. On Solana, composability is enhanced by the ability to pack multiple operations into a single fast, cheap transaction.
