## Order Book

An **order book** is a real-time list of all outstanding buy (bid) and sell (ask) orders for a trading pair, organized by price level. It shows the depth of supply and demand at each price point, helping traders understand market conditions.

### How It Works

Buy orders are listed from highest to lowest price, and sell orders from lowest to highest. The gap between the highest bid and lowest ask is called the spread. When a buy order matches a sell order at the same price, a trade executes. On Solana, protocols like Phoenix and OpenBook maintain fully on-chain order books with real-time matching.

### Why It Matters

Order books provide price discovery and transparency. They allow traders to see exactly where liquidity sits and place precise orders. On-chain order books on Solana combine the transparency of blockchain with the efficiency of traditional exchange matching, offering an alternative to AMM-based DEXs.
