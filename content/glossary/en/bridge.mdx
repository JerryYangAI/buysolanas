## Bridge

A **bridge** is a protocol that connects two or more blockchains, allowing users to transfer tokens, NFTs, and data between them. Bridges are essential infrastructure for a multi-chain crypto ecosystem.

### How It Works

Most bridges work by locking assets on the source chain and minting equivalent wrapped tokens on the destination chain. Some bridges use lock-and-mint mechanics, while others use liquidity pools on both sides for native asset swaps. Bridges are secured by various mechanisms including multisig validators, optimistic verification, or zero-knowledge proofs.

### Why It Matters

Bridges enable capital and users to flow between ecosystems. Without bridges, each blockchain would be an isolated island. However, bridges have been frequent targets for hackers due to the large amounts of locked assets they hold, making bridge security one of the most critical challenges in crypto.
