## Smart Contract Audit

A **smart contract audit** is a thorough review of a blockchain program's source code by security experts to identify bugs, vulnerabilities, and potential exploits before they can be used by attackers. It is a critical step in ensuring the safety of DeFi protocols and other on-chain applications.

### How It Works

During an audit, security professionals analyze the smart contract code line by line, looking for common vulnerabilities (like reentrancy attacks, integer overflow, or access control issues), logical errors, and potential economic exploits. They test edge cases and attack scenarios. The audit firm then produces a report documenting all findings, severity levels, and recommendations. The project team fixes the issues and may undergo a follow-up review.

### Why It Matters

Smart contract audits are one of the most important indicators of a project's legitimacy and security. Protocols that handle significant user funds should have at least one professional audit from a reputable firm. However, an audit is not a guarantee of safety — it only means the code was reviewed at a specific point in time, and new vulnerabilities can still emerge. When evaluating a Solana project, check whether it has been audited and by which firm.
