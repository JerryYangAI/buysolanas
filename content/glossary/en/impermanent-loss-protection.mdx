## Impermanent Loss Protection

**Impermanent loss protection** (ILP) is a feature offered by some DeFi protocols that compensates liquidity providers for the impermanent loss they experience due to price changes between the paired tokens.

### How It Works

Protocols that offer ILP track the impermanent loss each LP position accumulates over time. If an LP withdraws at a loss compared to simply holding, the protocol compensates them using protocol fees, treasury funds, or token emissions. Some protocols offer partial protection that increases over time, reaching full coverage after a certain period.

### Why It Matters

Impermanent loss is the primary risk for liquidity providers and often erodes or eliminates fee earnings. ILP makes liquidity provision more attractive by reducing this risk, encouraging deeper liquidity and better trading conditions. However, users should verify how ILP is funded, as it may be subsidized by token inflation.
