## Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

**Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)** is a security method that requires two different forms of identification before granting access to an account. In the crypto world, this typically means a password plus a time-based code from an authenticator app, adding a critical extra layer of protection to your accounts.

### How It Works

When 2FA is enabled, logging into your account requires two things: something you know (your password) and something you have (a temporary code from an app like Google Authenticator or Authy). These codes change every 30 seconds and are tied to your specific device. Even if an attacker discovers your password, they cannot access your account without also having your 2FA device.

### Why It Matters

For cryptocurrency users, enabling 2FA on every exchange account, email, and related service is essential. Many crypto thefts occur because attackers gain access to email accounts or exchange logins that lack 2FA. Always use an authenticator app rather than SMS-based 2FA, as phone numbers can be compromised through SIM-swapping attacks. While 2FA does not apply directly to blockchain wallets (which use private keys), it protects the centralized services that connect to your crypto activities.
