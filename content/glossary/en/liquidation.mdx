## Liquidation

**Liquidation** occurs in DeFi when a borrower's collateral value falls below the minimum required ratio. The protocol automatically sells enough collateral to repay part or all of the loan, often at a discount that rewards liquidators for maintaining system health.

### How It Works

Lending protocols set liquidation thresholds for each asset. When a borrower's health factor (collateral value relative to loan value) drops below 1, their position becomes eligible for liquidation. Third-party liquidator bots monitor the blockchain and execute liquidations, receiving a bonus (typically 5-10%) for their service.

### Why It Matters

Liquidation is the enforcement mechanism that keeps DeFi lending solvent. Without it, bad debts would accumulate and lenders could lose their deposits. Understanding liquidation risks is critical for borrowers — sudden price drops can trigger liquidation, resulting in loss of collateral plus a penalty fee.
