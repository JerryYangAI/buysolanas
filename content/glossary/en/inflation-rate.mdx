## Inflation Rate

The **inflation rate** of a cryptocurrency is the annual percentage at which new tokens are created and added to the supply. These new tokens typically go to validators and stakers as rewards for securing the network.

### How It Works

Solana started with an initial inflation rate of 8%, which decreases by 15% annually until reaching a long-term rate of 1.5%. New SOL is minted each epoch as staking rewards for validators and their delegators. Users who stake their SOL earn rewards that offset inflation, while non-stakers see their holdings diluted.

### Why It Matters

Inflation rate directly affects the real return on holding a token. If inflation is 5% and your tokens are not staked, your effective purchasing power decreases by 5% annually. Understanding inflation helps investors decide whether to stake and how to factor supply growth into their investment thesis.
