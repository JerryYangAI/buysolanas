## Copium

**Copium** is a portmanteau of "cope" and "opium," used humorously to describe the rationalizations people make to justify holding losing positions or explain away poor investment decisions. It implies someone is "inhaling copium" to feel better about losses.

### How It Works

When a token price drops significantly and holders create elaborate theories about why it will recover, others might say they are on copium. Common examples include cherry-picking positive indicators while ignoring bearish signals, or inventing conspiracies about price suppression.

### Why It Matters

While the term is used humorously, it highlights an important psychological trap in investing. Recognizing copium in yourself — the tendency to rationalize losses rather than objectively reassess — is valuable for making better investment decisions and managing risk appropriately.
