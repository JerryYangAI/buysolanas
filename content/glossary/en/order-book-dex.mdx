## Order Book DEX

An **order book DEX** is a decentralized exchange that uses a traditional order book to match buyers and sellers, rather than the liquidity pool model used by AMM DEXs. Traders place limit orders at specific prices, and the matching engine pairs compatible orders.

### How It Works

The order book maintains a list of all open buy and sell orders. When a buy order's price meets or exceeds a sell order's price, the trade executes. On Solana, projects like Phoenix and OpenBook run fully on-chain order books, leveraging Solana's speed to provide near-instant order matching and updates.

### Why It Matters

Order book DEXs offer advantages for active traders: precise price control, no impermanent loss for market makers, and familiar trading interfaces. Solana's high throughput and low latency make it uniquely suited for on-chain order books, which require fast, cheap transactions for competitive market making.
