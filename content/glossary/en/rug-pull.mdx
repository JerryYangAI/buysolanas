## Rug Pull

A **rug pull** is a type of cryptocurrency scam where the creators of a project suddenly withdraw all liquidity or funds and disappear, leaving investors with worthless tokens. The name comes from the expression "pulling the rug out from under someone."

### How It Works

In a typical rug pull, scammers create a new token and add liquidity to a DEX, making it appear tradeable. They generate hype through social media and marketing to attract buyers, driving up the price. Once enough money has flowed in, the scammers remove all the liquidity from the pool, drain the smart contract, or sell their massive token holdings. The token price crashes to zero, and buyers cannot sell their tokens because there is no remaining liquidity.

### Why It Matters

Rug pulls are one of the most common scams in crypto, especially on fast, low-cost chains like Solana where creating tokens is easy and cheap. To protect yourself, look for red flags: anonymous teams, no smart contract audits, locked liquidity (or lack thereof), unrealistic promises of returns, and aggressive marketing. Always research a project thoroughly before investing, and never put in more than you can afford to lose.
