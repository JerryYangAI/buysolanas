## Finality

**Finality** refers to the guarantee that a blockchain transaction, once confirmed, cannot be reversed, altered, or canceled. It is the point at which a transaction is considered permanently settled.

### How It Works

Different blockchains achieve finality differently. Bitcoin uses probabilistic finality — transactions become increasingly secure with each subsequent block but are never 100% final. Solana achieves optimistic confirmation in about 400ms and full finality in a few seconds through its Tower BFT consensus, making transactions effectively irreversible almost instantly.

### Why It Matters

Finality is crucial for financial applications. Fast finality means DEX trades, payments, and token transfers are quickly settled and secure. Slow or uncertain finality can enable double-spending attacks and creates risk for merchants and DeFi protocols that need to trust transaction permanence.
