## Cold Wallet

A **cold wallet** (also called cold storage or a hardware wallet) is a physical device or offline method for storing cryptocurrency private keys that never connects to the internet. This air-gapped approach provides the highest level of security against online threats like hacking, phishing, and malware.

### How It Works

Popular cold wallets like Ledger and Trezor are small USB-like devices that store your private keys on a secure chip. When you want to make a transaction, you connect the device to your computer, review and approve the transaction on the device's screen, and the device signs the transaction internally without ever exposing your private key to the internet. Even if your computer is compromised, attackers cannot access your keys.

### Why It Matters

Cold wallets are considered essential for anyone holding significant amounts of cryptocurrency. While hot wallets (like Phantom) are convenient for daily transactions, they are connected to the internet and therefore vulnerable to attacks. A common strategy is to use a hot wallet for everyday activities with small amounts and a cold wallet for long-term storage of larger holdings. This separation significantly reduces your risk of losing funds to hackers.
