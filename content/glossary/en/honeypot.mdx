## Honeypot

A **honeypot** is a type of crypto scam where a token's smart contract is programmed to allow users to buy but not sell. The token may look legitimate and even show a rising price on charts, but when holders try to sell, their transactions fail or get stuck. The name comes from the idea of a sweet trap that attracts victims.

### How It Works

Scammers deploy a token contract with hidden code that restricts selling — for example, by adding a 100% sell tax, blacklisting all addresses except the creator's, or including logic that blocks sell transactions entirely. The token gets listed on a DEX, appears on charts, and may even be promoted on social media. Victims buy in, see the price rising, but discover they cannot sell when they try to take profits.

### Why It Matters

Honeypots are particularly dangerous because they can look indistinguishable from legitimate tokens on the surface. On Solana, where new tokens are created constantly, honeypots are a persistent threat. Before buying any new or unknown token, check if others have been able to sell it successfully, use token scanner tools that analyze the contract code, and be extremely cautious with tokens that appear from nowhere with aggressive promotion.
