## Staking

**Staking** is the process of locking up your cryptocurrency tokens to help secure and operate a blockchain network. In return for staking, you earn rewards — typically paid in the same token you staked. On Solana, staking SOL helps validators process transactions and maintain the network's security.

### How It Works

When you stake SOL, you delegate your tokens to a validator — a computer that processes transactions and maintains the blockchain. Your tokens remain in your ownership but are locked for the duration of the staking period. The validator uses the combined stake from all delegators to participate in consensus, and the rewards earned are distributed proportionally. On Solana, staking rewards are typically around 6-8% APY.

### Why It Matters

Staking serves a dual purpose: it provides passive income for token holders and strengthens the network's security and decentralization. The more SOL that is staked across diverse validators, the more secure the network becomes. You can stake SOL directly through Phantom wallet, or use liquid staking protocols like Marinade or Jito to stake while keeping your assets liquid for use in DeFi.
