## Rebasing

**Rebasing** is a token mechanism where the protocol automatically adjusts the token supply in all wallets proportionally to target a specific price or growth rate. Positive rebases increase token balances, while negative rebases decrease them.

### How It Works

Rebase tokens have a target price (often $1). If the market price rises above the target, the protocol mints new tokens and distributes them proportionally to all holders (positive rebase). If the price falls below target, token balances are reduced (negative rebase). Your percentage ownership stays the same, but the number of tokens changes.

### Why It Matters

Rebasing was an experimental DeFi mechanism that gained attention with protocols like Ampleforth. While creative, rebasing tokens can be confusing and have largely fallen out of favor. Understanding them remains valuable for recognizing different tokenomics models and their trade-offs.
