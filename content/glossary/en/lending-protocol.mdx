## Lending Protocol

A **lending protocol** is a decentralized application that creates a marketplace for lending and borrowing crypto assets. Lenders deposit tokens to earn interest, while borrowers provide collateral to take out loans, all managed by smart contracts.

### How It Works

Lending protocols pool deposits from multiple lenders into smart contract vaults. Interest rates are determined algorithmically based on supply and demand — when borrowing demand is high, rates rise to attract more lenders. Borrowers must over-collateralize their loans, meaning they deposit more value than they borrow, protecting lenders from default.

### Why It Matters

Lending protocols are foundational DeFi infrastructure. They enable passive income for token holders, liquidity access for borrowers, and capital efficiency across the ecosystem. On Solana, protocols like marginfi and Solend provide these services with fast execution and low fees.
