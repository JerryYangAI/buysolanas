## Cross-Chain

**Cross-chain** refers to the technology and protocols that enable interactions between different blockchain networks. This includes transferring tokens, sharing data, and executing smart contract calls across separate chains.

### How It Works

Cross-chain communication relies on bridge protocols, messaging layers, and relay networks. When assets move cross-chain, they are typically locked on one chain and represented on another. More advanced cross-chain protocols enable arbitrary message passing, allowing smart contracts on different chains to coordinate actions.

### Why It Matters

No single blockchain can serve all use cases optimally. Cross-chain technology allows users to leverage the strengths of different networks — Solana's speed, Ethereum's security, and specialized chains' features — while maintaining a unified portfolio and seamless experience.
