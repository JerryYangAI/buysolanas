## Elastic Supply

**Elastic supply** refers to a token design where the total circulating supply adjusts automatically in response to market conditions. When demand is high, supply expands; when demand is low, supply contracts, with the goal of stabilizing the token price.

### How It Works

Elastic supply tokens use algorithms to monitor market price relative to a target. Supply adjustments can happen through rebasing (changing all wallet balances), minting new tokens as rewards, or burning tokens through buy pressure. The specific mechanism varies by protocol but always aims to create a self-correcting price system.

### Why It Matters

Elastic supply is an attempt to create tokens with more stable purchasing power without relying on collateral backing. While the concept has shown mixed results in practice, it represents an important area of monetary policy experimentation in decentralized finance.
