## Circulating Supply

**Circulating supply** is the number of tokens currently available to the public and actively trading on the market. It excludes tokens that are locked, vesting, burned, or otherwise removed from circulation.

### How It Works

Circulating supply includes all tokens held by the public, in liquidity pools, on exchanges, and in active wallets. It excludes tokens held in team vesting contracts, locked in staking with long unbonding periods, or allocated to the treasury but not yet distributed. Circulating supply is used to calculate market cap.

### Why It Matters

Circulating supply is critical for understanding a token's actual market dynamics. A token with a low circulating supply relative to total supply may face significant selling pressure as more tokens unlock. Checking the ratio of circulating to total supply helps investors assess dilution risk.
