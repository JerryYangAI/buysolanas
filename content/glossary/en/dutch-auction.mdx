## Dutch Auction

A **Dutch auction** is a pricing mechanism used in NFT mints where the price starts high and gradually decreases at fixed intervals until all NFTs are sold or a minimum price is reached.

### How It Works

The auction begins at a premium price and drops by a set amount at regular intervals (e.g., every 5 or 10 minutes). Buyers can mint at any point during the decline, and everyone who mints pays the same final clearing price — the price at which the last NFT sells. Some implementations refund the difference to early buyers.

### Why It Matters

Dutch auctions solve the price discovery problem for new NFT collections. Instead of guessing a fixed mint price, the market determines the fair value. They also reduce gas wars and bot competition since buyers can choose their entry point, and the declining price naturally spreads minting over time.
