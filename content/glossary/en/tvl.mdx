## TVL (Total Value Locked)

**Total Value Locked (TVL)** is a metric that measures the total dollar value of all cryptocurrency assets deposited in a DeFi protocol or blockchain. It is one of the most widely used indicators for gauging the size, popularity, and trustworthiness of a DeFi platform.

### How It Works

When users deposit tokens into a DeFi protocol — whether for lending, liquidity provision, staking, or other activities — those tokens are "locked" in the protocol's smart contracts. TVL sums up the value of all these deposits at current market prices. For example, if a lending protocol holds 1 million SOL and SOL is priced at $100, that protocol has a TVL of $100 million.

### Why It Matters

TVL helps you compare DeFi protocols and blockchains at a glance. A higher TVL generally indicates more user trust and deeper liquidity, which means better trading conditions and lower slippage. You can track Solana's TVL on sites like DeFiLlama to understand how the ecosystem is growing compared to other blockchains.
