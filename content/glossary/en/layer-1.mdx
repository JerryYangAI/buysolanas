## Layer 1 (L1)

A **Layer 1** blockchain is the base-level network that independently processes and finalizes transactions using its own consensus mechanism. Solana, Ethereum, and Bitcoin are all Layer 1 blockchains.

### How It Works

L1 blockchains maintain their own set of validators or miners, their own token for transaction fees, and their own consensus rules. All transactions are processed and finalized directly on the L1 chain. Each L1 makes different design trade-offs between decentralization, security, and scalability.

### Why It Matters

The L1 you choose determines your base transaction speed, cost, and security guarantees. Solana's L1 design prioritizes high throughput and low fees, enabling applications to run entirely on the base layer without needing additional scaling solutions that other L1s require.
