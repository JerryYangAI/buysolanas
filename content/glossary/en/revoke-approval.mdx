## Revoke Approval

**Revoking an approval** means removing a previously granted permission that allows a smart contract to spend your tokens. This is a critical security practice for protecting assets from potential exploits.

### How It Works

Using tools like revoke.cash or wallet-integrated features, users can view all active token approvals for their wallet and selectively revoke them. Revoking sends a transaction that sets the approval amount to zero, preventing the contract from spending any more tokens. This costs a small transaction fee.

### Why It Matters

Old, forgotten approvals are a common attack vector. Revoking unnecessary approvals reduces your attack surface. It is recommended to revoke approvals after completing interactions with a protocol, especially for less-trusted or experimental projects, and to periodically audit all active approvals.
