## Layer 2 (L2)

A **Layer 2** is a secondary framework or protocol built on top of a Layer 1 blockchain to improve its scalability. L2s process transactions off the main chain while inheriting its security, then settle the results back to the L1.

### How It Works

L2 solutions batch many transactions together and submit compressed proofs or summaries to the L1. This allows thousands of transactions to be represented by a single L1 transaction, dramatically reducing per-transaction costs. Common L2 types include rollups (optimistic and zero-knowledge), state channels, and sidechains.

### Why It Matters

L2s are primarily associated with Ethereum's scaling strategy, where high L1 fees make L2s necessary. Solana's high-throughput L1 design reduces the need for L2 scaling, though specialized L2 solutions may still be useful for specific applications requiring even higher throughput or privacy features.
