## Emission Rate

The **emission rate** is the speed at which new tokens are created and distributed within a blockchain ecosystem. Emissions typically go to validators, stakers, liquidity providers, or other participants who contribute to the network.

### How It Works

Emission rates are usually defined in the protocol's tokenomics and can be fixed (a set number of tokens per block) or variable (adjusting based on network conditions). Some protocols have decreasing emission schedules (like Bitcoin's halving), while others maintain steady emissions. On Solana, emission comes primarily through staking rewards, with the rate declining over time.

### Why It Matters

Emission rate determines how quickly the token supply grows and how much selling pressure exists from recipients of new tokens. High emission rates can suppress prices unless offset by strong demand. Investors should evaluate whether a protocol's emissions are sustainable and whether the value created justifies the dilution.
