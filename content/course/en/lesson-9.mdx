## SOL Staking Guide

Staking is one of the simplest ways to earn passive income with your SOL. By staking, you help secure the Solana network and receive rewards in return — typically around 6-8% annual yield. There are two main approaches: native staking and liquid staking.

### What Is Staking?

Solana uses a Proof of Stake consensus mechanism. Validators run powerful computers to verify transactions and produce blocks. When you stake your SOL with a validator, you are delegating your tokens to support their operations. In return, you receive a share of the rewards the validator earns.

### Native Staking

Native staking means delegating your SOL directly to a validator through your wallet. Here is how to do it in Phantom:

1. Open your Phantom wallet and click on your SOL balance.
2. Select "Start earning SOL" or navigate to the staking section.
3. Browse the list of validators and choose one.
4. Enter the amount of SOL you want to stake.
5. Confirm the transaction.

**Key details about native staking:**
- Rewards are distributed every epoch (approximately every 2-3 days).
- **Unstaking takes 2-3 days** — your SOL is locked during the cooldown period.
- You maintain full custody of your tokens; the validator cannot access them.
- There is no minimum staking amount (though very small amounts may not be practical).

### Liquid Staking

Liquid staking solves the lockup problem. When you liquid stake, you receive a derivative token that represents your staked position. This token can be used in DeFi while your original SOL continues earning staking rewards.

**Marinade Finance (mSOL):**
- Deposit SOL and receive mSOL in return.
- mSOL increases in value relative to SOL over time as staking rewards accrue.
- Use mSOL in DeFi protocols for additional yield.
- Marinade automatically distributes your stake across many validators, improving decentralization.

**Jito (JitoSOL):**
- Similar to Marinade but includes MEV (Maximal Extractable Value) rewards.
- MEV rewards come from the additional value validators capture from transaction ordering.
- JitoSOL historically offers slightly higher yields than standard staking.

### Comparing Staking Options

| Feature | Native Staking | Marinade (mSOL) | Jito (JitoSOL) |
|---------|---------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Approximate APY | 6-7% | 7-8% | 7-8.5% |
| Unstaking time | 2-3 days | Instant (via DEX) | Instant (via DEX) |
| DeFi composability | No | Yes | Yes |
| Validator choice | Manual | Automatic | Automatic |

### How to Choose a Validator (Native Staking)

If you choose native staking, selecting a good validator matters:

- **Commission rate** — lower is better for you (most charge 0-10%).
- **Uptime** — look for validators with 99%+ uptime.
- **Stake concentration** — consider staking with smaller validators to support network decentralization.
- **Track record** — check how long the validator has been operating.

### Risks and Considerations

- **Slashing** — Solana currently does not have slashing enabled, but it may be introduced in the future. Slashing penalizes validators (and their delegators) for misbehavior.
- **Validator risk** — a poorly performing validator may earn fewer rewards.
- **Smart contract risk** — liquid staking protocols carry additional smart contract risk.
- **Price risk** — staking rewards do not protect against SOL price decreases.
