## What Is Blockchain?

A blockchain is a **shared digital ledger** — think of it as a giant notebook that thousands of computers around the world keep in sync. Every time someone sends money or runs a program, it gets written into this notebook, and nobody can secretly change what is already there.

### Why Does It Matter?

Traditional finance relies on banks and payment companies to verify transactions. Blockchain removes the middleman. Instead, a network of computers (called **nodes**) verifies every transaction together, making the system:

- **Transparent** — Anyone can view the transaction history.
- **Secure** — Changing one record would require hacking thousands of computers simultaneously.
- **Permissionless** — No single company or government controls access.

### A Simple Analogy

Imagine a shared Google Spreadsheet that everyone in the world can read but no single person can edit alone. Every new entry needs approval from the majority of participants. Once approved, the entry is permanent. That is essentially how a blockchain works.

### How Does It Work?

1. **Someone initiates a transaction** — For example, Alice sends 1 SOL to Bob.
2. **The network verifies it** — Nodes check that Alice has enough funds and the transaction is valid.
3. **The transaction is grouped into a block** — Multiple transactions are bundled together.
4. **The block is added to the chain** — Once verified, the block is permanently linked to the previous block, forming a chain.

This chain of blocks is where the name "blockchain" comes from.

### Key Concepts to Remember

| Concept | What It Means |
|---------|--------------|
| **Block** | A bundle of verified transactions |
| **Node** | A computer that helps run the network |
| **Decentralized** | No single point of control or failure |
| **Immutable** | Once recorded, data cannot be altered |

### What Can You Do With Blockchain?

- **Send and receive money** — Without a bank, to anyone in the world, in seconds.
- **Use decentralized apps (dApps)** — Applications for trading, lending, gaming, and more.
- **Own digital assets** — Tokens, NFTs, and other items that you truly own.

### Key Takeaway

Blockchain is a technology that lets people transfer value and run applications without trusting a single authority. You do not need to understand every technical detail to get started — just like you do not need to know how the internet works to browse the web.
