## Liquidity Provision & Impermanent Loss

Providing liquidity is one of the primary ways to earn yield in DeFi. However, it comes with a unique risk called impermanent loss (IL). Understanding this concept deeply is essential before committing significant capital to liquidity pools.

### How Liquidity Provision Works

When you provide liquidity to a pool (e.g., SOL/USDC on Raydium or Orca), you deposit both tokens in a specific ratio. Traders who swap between these tokens pay a fee, and you earn a share of those fees proportional to your pool share.

**Concentrated Liquidity (CLMM):** Modern DEXs like Orca and Meteora use concentrated liquidity, where you choose a specific price range for your liquidity. This can dramatically increase fee earnings if the price stays within your range — but you earn nothing when the price moves outside it.

For example, if SOL is trading at $150, you might provide liquidity in the $130-$170 range. Swaps within this range generate fees for you, but if SOL moves to $180, your position is entirely converted to USDC and earns no fees until the price returns.

### Understanding Impermanent Loss

Impermanent loss occurs because the pool automatically rebalances your token ratio as prices change. If SOL doubles in price while you hold a SOL/USDC LP position, the pool sells some of your SOL for USDC to maintain balance. You end up with less SOL than if you had simply held it.

**Simplified IL calculation:**

| SOL Price Change | Impermanent Loss |
|-----------------|------------------|
| +25% | -0.6% |
| +50% | -2.0% |
| +100% (2x) | -5.7% |
| +200% (3x) | -13.4% |
| +400% (5x) | -25.5% |

The loss is "impermanent" because if the price returns to its original level, the loss disappears. It becomes permanent only when you withdraw your liquidity while prices have diverged.

### Strategies to Manage Impermanent Loss

**1. Choose correlated pairs:** Providing liquidity for pairs that move together (e.g., mSOL/SOL or USDC/USDT) significantly reduces IL since prices rarely diverge much.

**2. Focus on high-fee pools:** If a pool generates enough trading fees, the earnings can outweigh the impermanent loss. Volatile pairs like SOL/USDC often have high volume and fee generation.

**3. Use tight ranges wisely:** Concentrated liquidity in a narrow range earns more fees but requires active management. If you cannot monitor daily, use a wider range.

**4. Automated vaults:** Protocols like Kamino and Hawksight auto-rebalance your positions, keeping your liquidity in the active range without manual intervention.

**5. Calculate break-even:** Before entering a position, estimate the expected fees versus potential IL. Tools like the Meteora or Orca position calculators help with this.

### When LP Provision Makes Sense

- You believe the token pair will trade within a range (sideways market).
- The pool generates high trading volume relative to its liquidity.
- You are comfortable actively managing your position.
- The fee APR significantly exceeds potential IL.

### When to Avoid LP Provision

- You are extremely bullish on one token — holding outperforms LP in a strong uptrend.
- The pool has very low volume relative to liquidity (low fee generation).
- You cannot monitor or adjust your position regularly.
- You do not fully understand the risks involved.

### Tracking Your LP Performance

Use these tools to monitor your positions:

- **Orca analytics** — shows your position performance, fees earned, and current range.
- **Step Finance** — aggregates all your DeFi positions in one dashboard.
- **Manual tracking** — record your entry amounts and compare against simply holding.
