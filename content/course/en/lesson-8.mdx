## Solana DeFi Overview: Lending & Liquidity

Decentralized Finance (DeFi) lets you lend, borrow, and earn yield on your crypto without banks or middlemen. Solana's low fees and fast transactions make it one of the best chains for DeFi activity. Here is an overview of the major protocols.

### What Is DeFi Lending?

In traditional finance, banks accept deposits and lend them out, keeping most of the interest. In DeFi, smart contracts replace the bank. You deposit tokens into a lending protocol, borrowers pay interest to use them, and you earn a share of that interest — often at rates higher than traditional savings accounts.

### Marginfi

Marginfi is one of Solana's leading lending platforms. You can:

- **Supply assets** (SOL, USDC, USDT, and others) to earn interest.
- **Borrow against your deposits** — deposit SOL as collateral, then borrow USDC.
- **Monitor your health factor** — this number indicates how safe your position is from liquidation.

A health factor above 1.0 means you are safe. If it drops below 1.0, part of your collateral may be sold to repay the loan. Always maintain a comfortable margin.

### Kamino Finance

Kamino combines lending with automated liquidity strategies. Key features include:

- **Kamino Lend** — supply and borrow assets similar to Marginfi.
- **Automated vaults** — deposit tokens and let Kamino manage your liquidity position, auto-rebalancing to stay in range.
- **Multiply** — leverage your position by looping deposits and borrows.

Kamino is popular because it simplifies complex strategies into one-click vaults, reducing the manual work of managing positions.

### Raydium Liquidity Pools

Raydium is a major automated market maker (AMM) on Solana. When you provide liquidity to a Raydium pool, you deposit a pair of tokens (e.g., SOL and USDC) and earn a portion of the trading fees generated by swaps in that pool.

**How it works:**
1. Choose a trading pair (e.g., SOL/USDC).
2. Deposit equal value of both tokens.
3. Receive LP (Liquidity Provider) tokens representing your share.
4. Earn fees proportional to your share of the pool.

### Comparing Lending vs. Liquidity Provision

| Feature | Lending (Marginfi/Kamino) | Liquidity (Raydium) |
|---------|--------------------------|---------------------|
| Deposit | Single token | Token pair |
| Risk | Liquidation if borrowing | Impermanent loss |
| Yield source | Borrower interest | Trading fees |
| Complexity | Low to medium | Medium |

### Getting Started Safely

1. **Start small** — deposit a modest amount to understand how the protocol works.
2. **Check audits** — reputable protocols publish third-party security audits.
3. **Understand liquidation** — if you borrow, know your liquidation threshold.
4. **Diversify** — do not put all your funds into one protocol.
5. **Track your positions** — use dashboards like Step Finance to monitor everything in one place.

### Risks to Be Aware Of

- **Smart contract risk** — bugs in code can lead to loss of funds.
- **Oracle risk** — price feeds may be delayed or manipulated.
- **Impermanent loss** — relevant for liquidity provision (covered in a later lesson).
- **Regulatory risk** — DeFi regulations are evolving worldwide.
