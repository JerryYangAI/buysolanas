## Common Mistakes & How to Avoid Them

Crypto gives you full control of your money — but that also means there is no customer support to reverse mistakes. This lesson covers the most common pitfalls that trip up beginners and how to avoid every single one.

### Mistake 1: Sharing Your Seed Phrase

This is the number one way people lose their funds. Scammers will pose as support agents, create fake websites, or send urgent messages asking for your recovery phrase.

**The rule is simple:** No legitimate service, developer, or support team will ever ask for your seed phrase. If someone asks, it is a scam — 100% of the time.

### Mistake 2: Clicking Suspicious Links

Phishing attacks are extremely common in crypto. You might receive:

- **DMs on Discord or Telegram** claiming you won a prize
- **Fake airdrop announcements** linking to malicious websites
- **Emails pretending** to be from your exchange or wallet provider

**How to stay safe:**

- Never click links from strangers in DMs.
- Bookmark official websites and only access them through your bookmarks.
- Verify announcements through official Twitter/X accounts or websites.

### Mistake 3: Approving Malicious Transactions

When you connect your wallet to a dApp, it may ask you to approve transactions. Some malicious sites request permission to **drain your entire wallet**.

**Protection steps:**

- Read every transaction prompt carefully before signing.
- Use trusted dApps only — check community reviews and official links.
- Revoke unused token approvals regularly.
- Consider using a separate wallet with limited funds for exploring new apps.

### Mistake 4: Sending to the Wrong Network

Crypto exists on multiple networks. Sending SOL on the wrong network (like Ethereum's ERC-20 network instead of Solana) means your funds are likely gone forever.

**Always double-check:**

- The network you are sending on matches the receiving wallet.
- The wallet address is correct (copy-paste, do not type manually).
- You are sending the right token.

### Mistake 5: FOMO Investing

Fear of Missing Out leads many beginners to buy tokens that are spiking in price, only to watch them crash. Meme coins and new token launches are especially risky.

**Healthy habits:**

- Never invest money you cannot afford to lose.
- Research a project before buying — check the team, use case, and community.
- Be extremely skeptical of "guaranteed returns" or "100x" promises.
- Diversify instead of putting everything into one token.

### Mistake 6: Ignoring Security Hygiene

Small habits compound into serious protection:

- **Enable 2FA** on every exchange account (use an authenticator app, not SMS).
- **Keep your software updated** — wallet apps, browsers, and operating systems.
- **Use a dedicated browser** for crypto activities.
- **Never use public Wi-Fi** for wallet transactions.

### Quick Reference: Red Flags

| Red Flag | What It Really Is |
|----------|-------------------|
| "Send me 1 SOL, I will send back 10" | Scam |
| "Verify your wallet here" (link in DM) | Phishing |
| "Support agent" messaging you first | Impersonator |
| "Limited time — act now!" | Pressure tactic |
| Project with anonymous team and no audit | High risk |

### Key Takeaway

The best defense in crypto is a healthy dose of skepticism. Guard your seed phrase, verify every link and transaction, and never rush into decisions. Taking five extra seconds to double-check can save you from losing everything.
